Welcome To Dig This Volleyball

♦

Mission Statement: Dig This Volleyball is a community committed to developing and promoting the sport of volleyball from entry level to advanced player. Our goal is to provide an environment where we can support each other in the development of skills and sportsmanship, while providing a fun and nurturing place for players to reach their maximum playing potential.

Open Play - Indoor

✻

September 2016 - June 2017

Open Play for Advanced Beginners and Intermediate Players.

IMPORTANT - Skill Level Required for our Open Play Know how to rotate on the court, serve, pass, set, spike and block. Know how to hold your position on the court during defense and offense. This is meant for players that have experience playing. Rules will be enforced to protect all players from safety and fun. Recreational players and beginners can practice on the side or come watch for FREE. We also have individual coaching, please ask!

Meet The Team

✻

We promise you a new look and more importantly, a new attitude. We build that by getting to know you, your needs and creating the best looking clothes.

May

Co-Organizer, Event Host

One thing May loves about volleyball is that this is a team sport. You can also play this game without having to be awesome in all the skills, everyone has a specialty! She started Dig This Volleyball because of her passion for the sport. She loves supporting new players and enjoys competitive games when she’s not playing mom to her 3 kids.

Kathy

Co-Organizer, Event Host

Kathy has been an organizer of open play and tournaments for volleyball and basketball for the last few years. She also actively volunteers for New Yorkers Sports Association, a well reported local sports organization in the Chinese community. Since introduced to volleyball at the age of 13 years old, her commitment to advancing in the sport has never wavered. She has attended multiple volleyball camps and training clinics and strives to be the best player possible.

Amanda

Staff

Amanda been playing volleyball since the age of 10. She earned a starting position and captain position throughout middle school and high school. Not only have she played in middle school and high school she worked hard to earn a starting position as a Libero/Setter/Defensive Specialist for John Jay College of Criminal Justice Women's Volleyball team for 3 years. After graduating college, she continued to play in tournaments/leagues, pick up games and also volunteer her time helping former coaches with their boys and girls volleyball teams.

Reese

Staff

Reese was born, raised, and California-trained in volleyball. She is currently in her 2nd year of training under a Division 1 college coach on her club team. She is a multi-specialist in both Outside/Opposite and Setter. She has competed at the 2015 USAV Girls' Junior National Championships. Reese loves to not only play but coach as well. She’s happy to pass on all that she has learned to other enthusiastic players.

Marce

Staff

Marce has been playing volleyball since the age of 8. Volleyball runs through her family and has been passed down to her. She earned a starting and captain position throughout middle school and high school. She been coaching for 3 years at CYO volleyball team for St. Andrew Avellino.

Tony

Coach

Assistant women’s volleyball coach for Queensborough Community College. Assistant women’s volleyball coach of the Loyola University of New Orleans from 2006-2008. Tony has been involved with volleyball as a player and a coach for the past 20 years. His career began at 14 years of age while at Hippocrates High School in Kos, Greece.
